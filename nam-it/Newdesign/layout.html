About Willowbrook Farm subdivision

WF Homeowners Association has five elected board members. Positions are President, Secretary, Treasure and two members that manage compliances and Landscaping portfolios. Election for President and Treasurer occurs during every “even” years while Secretary and other positions during every odd years. This board meets every 1st Saturday of the month. Minutes of the meeting are available on website for home owners to review subjects of discussion.

Attention: New home owners:

WFHA welcomes you with pride to Willowbrook Farm community.While we wish you and your family a blissful home ownership, please do complete the following sooner.
1. Create your profile on WFHA website. (Steps) Share your general information with the Board of WFHA.
2. Remember your “Lot #. Your lot # will be your unique home id for all communication with the board
3. Review carefully the By-Laws and the Covenants to familiarize yourself on compliances - dos / don’ts’s
4. Let Novi police be aware that you are the new home owner, may seek help or guidance as appropriate
5. Let city of Novi “Public Works / Utilities dept.” know of your ownership, do seek updates as appropriate

Attention: if you are selling your home:

While WFHA wishes you & your family the best in your new abode, do ensure to complete the following:
1. Do let the Board of WFHA made aware once the sale of your home is complete in all respects, in time.
2. Do share information of new home owner, help the Board to welcome them / facilitate easy settling
3. Please share your new contcat information, help the Board reach out to you for any likely follow ups

Chat Window and Facebook page

To facilitate / share information and to communicate with ease a WFHA Facebook page has been created Also a “Chat window” been crated on the landing page of the website for easy and quick communication.

Rules of engagement for using Facebook and Chat window:

Facebook page as well the chat window are meant to share important updates and suggestions only.You may kindly avoid posting any messages that has a tone of discomfort / commercials / discriminations Sure we appreciate a collaborative participation and collective gain from these engagements.

Observe speed regulations while driving within the subdivision:

There are five “SLOW DOWN” signs within the sub. These signs help you to be cautious and maintain regulated speed limits while driving within the sub Speed limits are monitored by electronic sensors installed by Novi police at strategic locations within the sub, violators are be subjected to appropriate fines

Key information / compliance ready reckoner

Garbage clearance days / compliances (refer to By-Laws / Covenants for additional details) Waste Management is the authorized company to clear garbage on every Tuesdays. No clearances on national holidays. Clearance will be a day after the national days City of Novi collects cost of garbage as part of summer and winter property taxes Hence there will be no separate bill from Waste Management. Please be aware of this.Avoid leaving garbage / trash bins / yard waste sooner than prior evening to clearance days. Avoid leaving garbage bins on the front or on the side of your home, visible from the road.

Yard maintenance tips / compliances:

(refer to By-Laws / Covenants for additional details) Maintain your yard free of unwanted growth and weeds in line with guidance of By-law Trim encroaching tree branches over sidewalk that will not interfere with walkers or bike rider Remove grass clipping from sidewalk / driveway / street after completing lawn mowing Ensure to edge along the sidewalk on a regular basis to avoid grass from overgrowing

Repairs / maintenance of mail box / Street signs installed on your yard

Maintaining the condition of the mail box is the responsibility of the home owner. We have negotiated cost for all kinds of maintenance. For complete details click this link. Please be aware that protecting street signs installed on your yard from any damages will be the responsibility of the home owner. Cost of repair / replacement will be levied to home owner unless it is “proven” that the damage caused was by “unknown” individuals or by “city appointed contractors” either during snow ploughing or due to any unforeseen events.
